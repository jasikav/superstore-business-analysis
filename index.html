Superstore Business Performance Analysis

An end-to-end business analytics project analyzing retail sales data to uncover profitability drivers, operational gaps, and growth opportunities using Python and data visualization.

Executive Summary

This project analyzes transaction-level sales data from a retail superstore to evaluate overall business performance. The analysis focuses on sales trends, profitability, customer segments, and product categories to support data-driven decision-making.

Insights from this analysis highlight key revenue drivers, margin inefficiencies, and opportunities to improve long-term profitability through strategic pricing, product focus, and customer retention.

Key Business Metrics

  • Total Sales: ~$2.3 Million
  • Total Profit: ~$286 Thousand
  • Total Orders: ~5,000
  • Average Order Value: ~$458

Key Insights

  • Technology is the strongest performing category, generating both high sales and strong profit margins.
  • Furniture shows healthy revenue but weaker profitability, indicating pricing or discount inefficiencies.
  • Consumer customers contribute the highest order volume, while Corporate customers deliver better margins.
  • A small subset of products contributes disproportionately to total losses.

Business Recommendations

  • Increase marketing and inventory investment in Technology products.
  • Reassess discounting and pricing strategies within the Furniture category.
  • Develop targeted retention programs for high-value Corporate customers.
  • Identify and optimize or phase out consistently loss-making products.
